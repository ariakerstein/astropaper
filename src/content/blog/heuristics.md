There’s a few heuristics I like for understanding whether to persist or pivot on a particular thing: what is the quality of the people I’m engaged with on a daily basis (do I like, trust, admire them)? How hard/easy is growth - does the vision resonate? How proud will I be to get to the other side of hard?
And is it a win-win (for me)? The latter is particularly interesting. If the endeavor fails by whatever metric I had set out to optimize (revenue, growth etc.), is there another metric this still would likely have improved? For example: start a company and it doesn't succeed in the marketplace...did I learn something important about a new domain that I can carry forward? Did I acquire new skills? Work with great people I'd want to work with again?
Winning isn't always what we think it is. But setting the conditions to win happens across multiple dimensions that we actually do control
