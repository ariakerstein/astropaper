**Problem: overwhelm with details**
Do you find yourself drowning in the details? So many things? Maybe you think you have ADHD and need a diagnosis.

There will never be a shortage of details to process.
My co-founder calls this the red-queen syndrome - running in place and never getting anywhere. At Facebook, new PMs would get a poster with our values. My favorite was this one with a rocking horse. It does feel like sometimes we’re rocking hard. Back and forth, in place.

**It’s important to find a way through.**

There’s two reasons for this. Actually it’s just one, but it’s easy to think they are separate.
Effectiveness and joy. The reason I say they are one is because love is the bridge connecting these. There is a joy in the doing.

**How do we shift this tsunami of todo’s into a joy of doing?**

**Common methods ineffective people use**
Give up
Use a solution riddled with holes
A partial solution that isn’t thought all the way through.
Multiple apps - many systems.

If you care enough, then the details become part of the communing process with the object of your desire. It’s a good heuristic to figure out whether you are in fact in love. The idea:

In reflecting on past work experiences I noticed that I found deep joy in building products, talking to users and so on. And I hated the process of politicking around what it was I was doing. Actually I hated doing both at the same time. Both in isolation were just fine! Overwhelm.

Are you digging into the details, or are you avoiding them. If you find yourself delegating more than digging in that’s a good clue you are not in love with whatever it is you’re doing.   Your mileage may vary but that’s the case for me.
