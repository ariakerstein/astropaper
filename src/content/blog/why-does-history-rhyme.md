You'll often hear the refrain along the lines of: history doesn't repeat, but it rhymes. Why should that be?
I think there's 2 reasons for this.
The first is that we do not pass the lessons of history in any coherent way. We pass bits and pieces selected along many objectives. For example in America we squeeze US history into a few semesters covering a range of dates and events. But no distilled lessons. As such we carry a grab-bag of disconnected events with little by way of root causes.

So we are destined to relearn the lessons of history.

The second is that we as humans have a baseline of needs we are constantly satisfying.
